The origins of the Studebaker Corporation date back to 1852,
when brothers Henry and Clement Studebaker opened a blacksmith shop in South Bend,
Indiana. Studebaker eventually became a leading manufacturer of horse-drawn
wagons and supplied wagons to the U.S. Army during the Civil War.
